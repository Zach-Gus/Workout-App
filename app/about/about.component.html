Analisse Rios

La Paz, Bolivia

Education:

  • Cooperative School of La Paz, Bolivia (high school)
  • Connecticut College (graduated in 2008)
  • Southern Connecticut State University (masters 2011)

What do you enjoy most about working at Connecticut College?

I enjoy working with and connecting with athletes and coaches. I also love being on campus and seeing some of the professors or staff members I knew from when I went to Conn!

How did you get to where you are today as being the Women's Assistant Soccer Coach and Strength and Conditioning Coach for all sports here at Connecticut College?

I graduated from Connecticut College in 2008 and immediately began teaching in New London. I was a 5th and 6th grade teacher for 10 years. In the spring of 2016, I realized that I missed being part of a team and missed soccer. I contacted Norm Riker (Head Coach Women’s Soccer) and asked if I could help the team in any way. During that spring, I created workouts for the soccer team and would take them on trail runs. The next season, my role continued and I would go to practices and games with the team. That spring, I realized that I really enjoyed coaching and would love to get into the strength and conditioning world. I pursued a few different certifications over the next couple of years and now I’m here - working with 7 different teams!

Why did you Choose Connecticut College?

For college: I remember researching different colleges in the United States and coming across Connecticut College because of their elementary education certification program. I knew I wanted to be a teacher and play D3 soccer so I looked into it a bit more. I loved the honor code and everything the college stood for so I decided to apply early decision. My first day on campus (and in CT for that matter) was my freshmen move in day! For work: I had a wonderful experience as a student at Connecticut College and once I started getting involved with the soccer team, I knew I ultimately wanted to work here. It all came full circle, I guess!

What do you enjoy most about working with athletes?

I love seeing athletes work hard and strive to become better every single time they enter the weight room or start their practice. I love the dedication they bring with them and the perseverance they have. I also really enjoy making connections with them, learning from them, and hopefully inspiring them.

Favorite healthy snack after a workout?
